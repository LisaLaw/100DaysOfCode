Hello! My name is Lisa and I am a auto-didactic web developer.
I have found my passion for programming through trial and error.

My original field of studies was Linguistics, which I decided to develop into Computational Linguistics (or NLP).
I had the fantastic opportunity to spend a year at UCLA, home of Computational Linguistics in the US and benefitted from some incredible teachers and courses, which first sparked my interest.

But I wasn't ready to make the jump then. I worked as a Project Manager in translations for a while which taught me multitasking and what it's like to work in a fast-paced environment.
Next, I completed a Master's level programme in education, a Post Graduate Certificate in Secondary Foreign Language Teaching (PGCE) and before I knew it, I was working with teenagers. An invaluable experience which I wouldn't have missed for any price, this taught me patience and looking at a problem from every point of view.
During this time, I strongly felt a need to create, to develop and to work with challenging, analytical tasks.
I researched Master programmes in programming and / or NLP but in the end decided to teach myself.

I have some experience teaching myself as I have a love for languages and have previously learned Spanish and French mostly by myself. I love working on a new language - be it spoken or coding -, working out the intrinc details and logic structures that underlie them.

So at the beginning of 2020 I started my Python journey with Coursera courses and have been hooked every since.
A short while later I also started refreshing my HTML, CSS and JavaScript. In May I started the 100 Day of Code - Challenge.
I have been coding every day since and I'm learning and loving it.
